# Infusion Diary – SCIG Patient App
### Instructions for healthcare professionals and patients

---

## For healthcare professionals – sharing the app with patients

The app is a web-based infusion diary for subcutaneous immunoglobulin (SCIG) treatment. It runs in any mobile browser and can be saved to the patient's home screen like a regular app.

**Share this link with your patient:**
```
https://kjellanker.github.io/Infusjonsdagbok/infusjonsdagbok.html
```

Send it by SMS, email, or messaging app. No installation required.

## For patients – getting started

### Step 1 – Open the app
Open the link you received in **Safari** (iPhone) or **Chrome** (Android).

### Step 2 – Add to your home screen
**iPhone (Safari):**
1. Tap the share icon (square with arrow) at the bottom of the screen
2. Scroll down and tap **Add to Home Screen**
3. Tap **Add** — the app icon now appears on your home screen

**Android (Chrome):**
1. Tap the three-dot menu in the top right
2. Tap **Add to Home Screen**
3. Tap **Add**

## Using the app

The app has three sections, accessible from the bar at the bottom:

### Logbook
Your main diary. Tap **+ New** to register an infusion. Fill in:
- **Date** – defaults to today
- **Dose** – enter the amount and select g or ml
- **Batch number (LOT)** – found on the packaging
- **Side effects** – leave blank if none
- **Other** – any additional notes

Entries with side effects are marked with a warning icon in the list.

### Visits
Record your clinic appointments. Tap **+ New** and fill in date, time, the name of your doctor or nurse, and any notes agreed upon during the visit.

### Info
Enter your personal details, contact persons, and medication information. This is filled in once and stays saved. Tap **Save** when done.

---

## Exporting your data

Your data is stored locally on your device and is not sent anywhere. To back it up or share it with your clinic:

1. Go to the **Info** tab
2. Tap the **download icon** in the top right corner
3. A file is saved to your device containing all your entries
4. Open the file in your browser and tap **Print / Save as PDF**

Bring the PDF to your clinic appointment, or save it to your cloud storage as a backup.

> **Note:** If you change phone or clear your browser data, your diary entries will be lost unless you have exported them first. Export regularly to keep a backup.

---

## Privacy

All data is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server. The app has no login and no tracking.
